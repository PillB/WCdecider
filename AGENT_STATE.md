## 🛑 Immutable Constraints & Guardrails
- “Complete” means complete for a declared source/scope contract, not every bookmaker and match ever played. Coverage denominators and source limitations must be published.
- A closing price is the last verifiable pre-kickoff quote from a named source. Post-kickoff, opening-only, aggregated-without-timestamp, estimated, interpolated, or model-implied prices cannot be labeled closing odds.
- Respect source licenses, robots rules, rate limits, authentication, and redistribution terms. Do not bypass access controls or scrape prohibited sources.
- Separate raw immutable acquisitions from normalized and derived tables; every row must retain lineage to the raw source payload and SHA-256.
- Historical odds used for evaluation must be temporally available before each kickoff and isolated from model/threshold selection to prevent look-ahead and selection leakage.
- Top-four ranking cannot duplicate equivalent selections or manufacture prices. A card with fewer than four supported sourced candidates must disclose the source-coverage shortfall instead of filling ranks with invented bets.
- Model-championship search, ensemble weighting, calibration, and policy thresholds must be nested inside chronological folds; the final holdout cannot be used to choose the champion.
- Every phase/turn follows READ → ACT → WRITE & COMPRESS; `AGENT_STATE.md` remains in this exact schema.
- Never fabricate odds, source data, match results, probabilities, or validation success. Unreadable or unavailable evidence is explicitly flagged.
- Preserve the current screenshot deletion/addition set and unrelated untracked files as user-owned; do not restore, discard, or overwrite unrelated changes.
- Prediction scope is June 22–27, 2026 only. Earlier matches are historical training/backtest inputs.
- Odds must come from actual `Screenshots/` images, not hard-coded dictionaries, prose, remembered prices, or placeholder filenames.
- Every externally sourced value requires a direct URL, retrieval timestamp, and documented transformation.
- Model selection and calibration must use chronological folds with no full-sample selection leakage; missing required weights are fatal.
- No “sure-fire” or guaranteed-win claims. Confidence is capped at 70%; recommendations include uncertainty, failure scenarios, and responsible-gambling language.
- The user requires one recommendation per match. Recommendations without current source odds are model-side watchlist actions with fair-price thresholds, not executable sportsbook-price or profitability claims.
- Current-match UI must have exactly one JSON-driven card per canonical fixture and complete English/Spanish visible content.
- Never mark a gate complete without command output, artifact validation, or independent review evidence.
- Every published model datapoint must be represented in an audit manifest with source hashes, model version, responsible subagent mission/result, replication status, and editorial synchronization status; aggregate review without datapoint linkage is insufficient.
- Missing odds never become estimated prices: fixtures without a real supported market price receive probability/fair-price analysis only.
- Combo probabilities must come from a joint score distribution and exact conjunction settlement, never naive multiplication of marginal probabilities.
- Betsson handicap-plus-total headers apply the displayed signed handicap to selection 1/home and the opposite handicap to selection 2/away; this rule is supported by visually checked negative and positive examples and must remain source-documented.
